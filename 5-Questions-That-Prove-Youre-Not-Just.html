The Problem: You've Been Taught to Minimize Yourself

You've been taught to use service verbs. They sound humble. Professional. Appropriate for "women's work."

But here's what they actually do: They hide your accountability. And accountability determines pay.

When you say you "assist with" or "support" or "help coordinate," you're telling your employer (and yourself) that someone else is really in charge. That you're just... helping.

Even when you're the one who would be blamed if it all fell apart.

How This Actually Works (And It's Not What You Think)

The work that women naturally do‚Äîorganizing, coordinating, facilitating, making sure everything actually runs‚Äîhas already been classified as "service" or "support" work. Before she even walks in the door.

A Bit of History (Because This Matters):

When women were finally "allowed" into office work in the 1950s and 60s, they were funneled into newly created roles: secretaries, typists, receptionists, coordinators.

These roles were literally designed to be subordinate to male executives. The language reflected that: "assist the manager," "support the executive," "coordinate schedules," "facilitate communications."

That linguistic classification system‚Äîcreated 70 years ago to describe women's work as subordinate office support‚Äîbecame the TEMPLATE that's still being used today.

Even as the actual work evolved into full management responsibility, the titles stayed stuck. And the pay scales stayed tied to those titles.

Meanwhile, the exact same work‚Äîwhen it's done in a traditionally male-dominated field or framed as "execution" rather than "coordination"‚Äîgets titled "Manager" or "Operations Lead" or "Project Director."
